Improving Checkout Flows

Reducing friction throughout our checkout flows in order to increase conversions

Project Overview

Overview

We were tasked with optimizing the checkout flow for our SaaS product to address several key business objectives. These objectives included increasing revenue, improving conversion rates, and generating leads from users who chose not to complete their checkout. The existing flow was functional but lacked optimization for a streamlined, intuitive user experience, resulting in cart abandonment and missed opportunities to engage potential customers.

My role

Head of Product Design & Project Lead
To lead the design team as well as act as product owner. This entailed conducting research, user journey mapping, analytic analysis, and cross-team collaboration to maintain feasibility.

Project Objectives

Increase Revenue:
By making the checkout flow more efficient and user-friendly, the goal was to boost the number of completed purchases.

Improve Conversion Rates:
Streamlining the steps would reduce friction in the process and guide users more naturally toward completing their purchase.

Generate Leads:
For users who didn't convert, I aimed to introduce mechanisms to capture lead information for future nurturing.

Minimize Friction:
Reduction of forms and unnecessary information causing friction in the sign-up flow to maintain and enhance user acquisition.

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge
Solution

High Abandonment Rates

Since users were leaving at critical steps of the checkout, such as during the payment phase, we minimized friction and removed unnecessary steps resulting in a more seamless user experience.

Complex Checkout Process

The previous flow had multiple steps and asked for too much information upfront, overwhelming potential customers. To solove this, we reduced the amount of fields the user would have to enter in order to sign up by over 50%.

Poor Mechanism for Re-Engagement

Since there was a poor mechanism to re-engage users who abandoned their carts, leading to lost opportunities for follow-ups. We decided to collect contact information at the beginning of the flow that makes re-engagment a lot easier if they fall off anywhere after that step.

Confusing Display for important information

The previous flow had a very confusing and distracting ways to display important information especially revolving around pricing and future charges. To solve this we created easy-to-understand ledger style summaries surrounding the pricing and charges making it extremely clear when and what they will be charged.

Outcomes & Results

Increased User Satisfaction

User feedback indicated a clear understanding of the new pricing model, with minimal complaints during the transition period.

Smooth Transition

Legacy users transitioned to the new pricing model without issues, thanks to the grandfathering option and support provided.

Stakeholder Alignment

Stakeholders were satisfied with the seat-based pricing model after seeing the positive impact on user acquisition and retention.

Higher Conversion Rates

The optimized sign-up flow resulted in a 15% increase in sign-up completion rates.

The project was a success, demonstrating the critical role of UX/UI design in navigating complex pricing changes and enhancing user experience. By focusing on clear communication, user-centric design, and continuous optimization, we were able to achieve a smooth transition and drive business growth. This case study underscores the importance of aligning design strategies with business objectives and user needs.
