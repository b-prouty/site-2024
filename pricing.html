Pricing Structure

Transitioning to Seat Based Pricing

Adding pricing flexibility to a product built to save HVAC companies time and money.

Overview

As the Senior UX/UI Designer at Bluon, I was tasked with leading the redesign of our SaaS product's pricing structure. Our company decided to shift from a price bucket based pricing model to a seat based pricing model. This change was aimed at aligning our pricing strategy more closely with user value and driving revenue growth. The project involved multiple challenges, including effectively communicating the pricing changes, addressing the needs of both legacy and new users, navigating stakeholder preferences, and reducing friction in the sign-up flow.

My role

Head of Product Design & Project Lead
Team management and Interaction & Visual Design, Cross-team collaboration

Project Objectives

  • Communicate the new seat-based pricing structure clearly to users.
  • Ensure a smooth transition for legacy users from the old pricing model to the new one.
  • Balance stakeholder desires for usage-based pricing with the decision to implement seat-based pricing.
  • Minimize friction in the sign-up flow to maintain and enhance user acquisition.

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge

Explaining the new seat-based pricing model in a way that was easy for users to understand and accept.

Solution

We implemented a multi-channel communication strategy. This included:

  • In-App Notifications: Informative pop-ups were designed to notify existing users about the upcoming changes well in advance.
  • Email Campaigns: Detailed emails were sent out, highlighting the benefits of the new pricing structure and how it would affect their billing.

The design of these communications focused on clarity and simplicity, using visuals and examples to illustrate the new pricing model.

Challenge

Ensuring that legacy users were transitioned smoothly to the new pricing model without disrupting their current usage or causing dissatisfaction.

Solution

We provided legacy users with a grandfathering option, where they could continue with their current pricing for a specified period before transitioning to the new model. Additionally, a dedicated support team was made available to assist users with any concerns during the transition.

We also made sure that each legacy customer got a one-on-one phone call with our team to easily understand how the transition would affect them, including cost comparisons and seat allocation management.

Challenge

Navigating the preference from some stakeholders for a usage-based pricing model instead of a seat-based one.

Solution

To address stakeholder concerns, we conducted extensive user research and competitive analysis to demonstrate the advantages of seat-based pricing. We highlighted that seat-based pricing was more predictable and easier for users to understand and manage.

In our design presentations, we showcased the potential revenue impact and user satisfaction improvements with seat-based pricing, gaining buy-in from key stakeholders by aligning our UX strategy with business goals.

Challenge

Minimizing friction in the sign-up flow to ensure high conversion rates.

Solution

We redesigned the sign-up flow with a focus on simplicity and clarity. Key changes included:

  • Streamlined Form Design: Reducing the number of fields in the sign-up form to the essential minimum, and implementing smart defaults and auto-fill options.
  • Pricing Transparency: Adding our pricing as soon as their info was entered to help users under stand our pricing structure, reducing the amount of page bounces trying to compare rates.
  • Clear Value Proposition: Highlighting the benefits of the pricing model prominently on the checkout page to reinforce the value users would receive.

We also introduced A/B testing to continuously optimize the sign-up flow based on user behavior and feedback.

Outcomes & Results

Increased User Satisfaction

User feedback indicated a clear understanding of the new pricing model, with minimal complaints during the transition period.

Smooth Transition

Legacy users transitioned to the new pricing model without issues, thanks to the grandfathering option and support provided.

Stakeholder Alignment

Stakeholders were satisfied with the seat-based pricing model after seeing the positive impact on user acquisition and retention.

Higher Conversion Rates

The optimized sign-up flow resulted in a 15% increase in sign-up completion rates.

The project was a success, demonstrating the critical role of UX/UI design in navigating complex pricing changes and enhancing user experience. By focusing on clear communication, user-centric design, and continuous optimization, we were able to achieve a smooth transition and drive business growth. This case study underscores the importance of aligning design strategies with business objectives and user needs.
